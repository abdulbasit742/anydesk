# RemoteDesk Privacy Policy Documentation

## Introduction
This document outlines the RemoteDesk Privacy Policy, detailing how we collect, use, process, and protect the personal data of our users. RemoteDesk is committed to safeguarding your privacy and ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations, including GDPR, CCPA, and other relevant frameworks.

## 1. Data We Collect

RemoteDesk collects various types of information to provide and improve our services. The specific data collected depends on how you interact with our platform.

### 1.1. Information You Provide to Us
-   **Account Information:** When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, organization name, and contact details.
-   **Billing Information:** For paid services, we collect billing address, payment method details (processed securely by third-party payment processors), and transaction history.
-   **Support Communications:** Records of your communications with our support team, including emails, chat transcripts, and phone calls.
-   **User Content:** Any information you provide when using our features, such as notes, chat messages within sessions, or feedback.

### 1.2. Information We Collect Automatically
-   **Device Information:** Information about the devices you use to access RemoteDesk, including IP address, operating system, device type, and unique device identifiers.
-   **Usage Data:** Details about how you use our services, such as session duration, features accessed, pages viewed, and actions taken. This includes data defined by `ApiUsageMetricSchema` and `UsageSummarySchema`.
-   **Log Data:** Server logs that may include your IP address, browser type, referring/exit pages, and timestamps.
-   **Session Data:** Metadata about remote sessions, such as start/end times, connected devices, and network performance. This does NOT include the content of your remote sessions unless explicitly enabled by your organization and with appropriate consent (see `SessionRecordingConsentSchema`).

### 1.3. Information from Third Parties
-   We may receive information from third-party services integrated with RemoteDesk (e.g., SSO providers, marketplace applications) or from partners (see `PartnerAccountSchema`). This data is handled according to the respective third-party privacy policies and our agreements with them.

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for various purposes, primarily to provide, maintain, and improve our services.

-   **To Provide Services:** To enable remote access, manage user accounts, process payments, and deliver core RemoteDesk functionalities.
-   **To Improve and Personalize Services:** To understand usage patterns, troubleshoot issues, develop new features, and personalize your experience.
-   **For Security and Compliance:** To protect against fraud, enforce our terms of service, comply with legal obligations, and maintain the security of our platform. This includes enforcing `DlpAuditEventSchema` and `PrivacySettingsSchema`.
-   **For Communication:** To send service-related notifications, updates, security alerts, and marketing communications (where permitted and with your consent).
-   **For Analytics and Research:** To perform data analysis, research, and reporting to understand market trends and improve our offerings.

## 3. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

-   **With Your Consent:** We may share information when you give us explicit permission to do so.
-   **With Service Providers:** We engage third-party service providers to perform functions on our behalf (e.g., hosting, payment processing, analytics). These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data and use it only for the purposes for which it was disclosed.
-   **With Your Organization:** If you use RemoteDesk through an enterprise account, your organization’s administrator may have access to your usage data and session metadata.
-   **For Legal Reasons:** We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or other legal process, or if we believe it is necessary to protect our rights, property, or safety, or the rights, property, or safety of others.
-   **Business Transfers:** In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of the transaction.

## 4. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. The duration of retention is influenced by factors such as the nature of the data, the purpose of processing, and legal obligations. Our `PrivacySettingsSchema` includes `auditLogRetentionDays`.

## 5. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

-   **Right to Access:** Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
-   **Right to Rectification:** Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
-   **Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten):** Request deletion of your personal data under certain conditions.
-   **Right to Restriction of Processing:** Request that we limit the way we use your personal data.
-   **Right to Data Portability:** Request to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
-   **Right to Object:** Object to the processing of your personal data under certain circumstances.
-   **Right to Withdraw Consent:** Withdraw your consent at any time where we rely on consent to process your personal data.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details provided in Section 7.

## 6. Security Measures

RemoteDesk implements robust technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. These measures include:

-   **Encryption:** Data encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest (AES-256).
-   **Access Controls:** Strict access controls and least privilege principles for internal systems.
-   **Regular Security Audits:** Periodic security audits, penetration testing, and vulnerability assessments.
-   **Employee Training:** Regular security awareness training for all employees.

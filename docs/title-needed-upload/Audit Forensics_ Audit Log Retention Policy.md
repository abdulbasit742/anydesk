# Audit/Forensics: Audit Log Retention Policy

This document defines the policy for the retention and archival of audit logs generated by the RemoteDesk system. A clear log retention policy is essential for compliance with regulatory requirements, supporting forensic investigations, and ensuring the availability of historical data for security and operational analysis.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:

*   Ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and industry standards (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS, SOC 2).
*   Provide sufficient historical data for security incident response and forensic analysis.
*   Support troubleshooting and root cause analysis of operational issues.
*   Optimize storage costs by defining appropriate retention periods for different log types.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all audit logs generated by RemoteDesk applications (Web, Desktop, Backend API, Signaling Server) and infrastructure components that record user actions, system events, and security-relevant information, as defined in `audit-log-structure.md`.

## 3. Log Categories and Retention Periods

Audit logs are categorized based on their criticality and the data they contain. Different categories may have different retention periods.

| Log Category | Description | Retention Period (Online/Hot Storage) | Retention Period (Archival/Cold Storage) | Notes |
| :----------- | :---------- | :------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------------- | :---- |
| **Security Events** | User logins/logouts, authentication failures, access control changes, critical system configuration changes. | 90 days | 1 year | Critical for forensic investigations and compliance. |
| **Session Activity** | Session start/end, remote input events, screen sharing events, file transfer initiation/completion. | 30 days | 6 months | Useful for troubleshooting user-reported issues and activity monitoring. |
| **System Events** | Application startup/shutdown, service health checks, non-critical errors, system configuration changes. | 14 days | 3 months | Primarily for operational monitoring and debugging. |
| **Billing/Transactional** | Events related to billing, subscription changes, payment processing. | 7 years | 7 years | Strict compliance requirements (e.g., financial regulations). |

## 4. Retention Mechanism

*   **Centralized Logging System:** All audit logs are ingested into a centralized logging system (e.g., ELK Stack, Splunk, cloud-native logging services).
*   **Tiered Storage:** The logging system will utilize tiered storage solutions to manage costs and access speed:
    *   **Hot Storage:** For frequently accessed logs (e.g., last 30-90 days) for real-time monitoring and immediate troubleshooting.
    *   **Cold Storage/Archival:** For long-term retention of older logs that are less frequently accessed but required for compliance or deep forensic analysis.
*   **Automated Lifecycle Management:** Storage lifecycle policies will be configured to automatically transition logs from hot to cold storage and eventually delete them after their defined retention period.

## 5. Log Immutability and Integrity

*   **Write-Once, Read-Many (WORM):** Logs, once written to the logging system, must be immutable. Mechanisms will be in place to prevent alteration or deletion of logs before their retention period expires.
*   **Hashing/Digital Signatures:** Optionally, logs can be cryptographically hashed or digitally signed to provide verifiable proof of their integrity and detect any tampering.
*   **Access Control:** Strict access controls will be enforced on the logging system to ensure only authorized personnel can view or manage logs.

## 6. Compliance and Legal Hold

*   **Compliance Officer:** A designated compliance officer or team will be responsible for overseeing adherence to this policy and relevant regulations.
*   **Legal Hold:** In the event of litigation, investigation, or other legal requirements, specific logs or categories of logs may be placed on legal hold, overriding standard retention periods until the hold is released.

## 7. Policy Review

This Audit Log Retention Policy will be reviewed annually, or as required by changes in legal/regulatory requirements or business needs, to ensure its continued effectiveness and relevance.

# RemoteDesk Vendor Risk Assessment Template

## Introduction
This template is designed to assess and manage the risks associated with third-party vendors and service providers utilized by RemoteDesk. Effective vendor risk management is critical for maintaining information security, ensuring compliance, and protecting organizational assets. This document facilitates a structured approach to evaluating vendor security posture, operational practices, and compliance adherence.

## Assessment Areas

### 1. Information Security Management
-   [ ] **Information Security Policy:** Does the vendor have a documented information security policy?
-   [ ] **ISMS Certification:** Is the vendor ISO 27001 certified or equivalent?
-   [ ] **Security Team:** Does the vendor have a dedicated security team?
-   [ ] **Security Awareness Training:** Do vendor employees receive regular security awareness training?

### 2. Data Protection and Privacy
-   [ ] **Data Encryption:** Is data encrypted at rest and in transit?
-   [ ] **Data Residency:** Does the vendor comply with data residency requirements?
-   [ ] **Privacy Policy:** Does the vendor have a clear privacy policy?
-   [ ] **GDPR/CCPA Compliance:** Is the vendor compliant with relevant data privacy regulations?
-   [ ] **Data Breach Notification:** Does the vendor have a data breach notification process?

### 3. Access Control
-   [ ] **Least Privilege:** Does the vendor enforce the principle of least privilege for access to RemoteDesk data/systems?
-   [ ] **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Is MFA enforced for administrative access to vendor systems?
-   [ ] **Access Reviews:** Does the vendor conduct regular access reviews?
-   [ ] **Segregation of Duties:** Is segregation of duties implemented for critical functions?

### 4. Network Security
-   [ ] **Firewall/IDS/IPS:** Does the vendor utilize firewalls, Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), and Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS)?
-   [ ] **Network Segmentation:** Is network segmentation implemented to isolate sensitive systems?
-   [ ] **Vulnerability Management:** Does the vendor have a vulnerability management program (scanning, patching)?

### 5. Application Security
-   [ ] **Secure Development Lifecycle (SDLC):** Does the vendor follow a secure SDLC?
-   [ ] **Security Testing:** Does the vendor perform regular security testing (e.g., penetration testing, vulnerability assessments) on their applications?
-   [ ] **Input Validation:** Are inputs validated to prevent common web vulnerabilities (e.g., XSS, SQL Injection)?

### 6. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery
-   [ ] **BC/DR Plan:** Does the vendor have a documented and tested Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery plan?
-   [ ] **Backup and Recovery:** Are data backup and recovery procedures in place and regularly tested?
-   [ ] **RTO/RPO:** Are Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO) defined and met?

### 7. Incident Management
-   [ ] **Incident Response Plan:** Does the vendor have a documented incident response plan?
-   [ ] **Incident Reporting:** What is the process for reporting security incidents to RemoteDesk?
-   [ ] **Forensics Capabilities:** Does the vendor have capabilities for forensic analysis in case of an incident?

### 8. Compliance and Audit
-   [ ] **Compliance Certifications:** Does the vendor hold relevant certifications (e.g., SOC 2 Type 2, HIPAA, PCI DSS)?
-   [ ] **Audit Rights:** Does the contract include audit rights for RemoteDesk?
-   [ ] **Sub-processor Management:** How does the vendor manage risks associated with their sub-processors?
